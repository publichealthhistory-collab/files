About the project

Public Health History is a non-commercial public history project that explores how societies around the world have understood, addressed and contested health, disease and wellbeing over time.

The project uses historical case studies, sites and ideas to examine public health not as a simple story of linear progress, but as a field shaped by power, inequality, culture and contested values. It aims to use historical perspective to inform contemporary public health thinking and practice.

About the author

The series is created by Dr Albie Sharpe, a Lecturer in Public Health at the University of Technology Sydney.

His work focuses on health promotion, global health and community-based research, with particular attention to the social determinants of health, equity, participation and the role of historical context in shaping public health policy and practice.
